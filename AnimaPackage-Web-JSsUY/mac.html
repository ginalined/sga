Machu Picchu: The most dangerous "land of the sky" I have been to

Machu Picchu is in Peru, on the ridge 130 km from Cusco, with an ancient ruin surrounded by dense rainforest. This is Machu Pichu, according to archaeologists, this is the remains of the Inca Empire that existed in Peru during the pre-Columbian period (around 1492).

Arrive at the town of Ollantaytambo in the afternoon, the same passengers are on the train here, only I choose this non-mainstream way to go. I learned that
the bus to Santa Teresa departed at 3 pm. Due to time, I took a taxi and sent me directly to the pedestrian entrance along the train.

The entire architecture of Machu Picchu is traditional in Inca: polished regular-shaped walls, and wonderful seaming techniques. The gap between the stone and the stone on the wall cannot be put into the capital, making it impossible for people to Understand how the Incas are splicing them together.














It is interesting to note that although the Incas understand the circle (the Sun God uses it for expression), it does not apply it to buildings. It is still a mystery how the huge amount of stones used in construction has been carried. Also, although the Incas did not use a circle, they used the slope. They are believed to have tens of thousands of workers pushing stones to climb the slopes. It is a pity that the Incas did not master the skills of the text without leaving any descriptive text.

The entire ruins consist of approximately 140 buildings, including temples , shelters , parks and residential areas . There are more than 100 steps built here - each usually made of a whole piece of huge granite. There are also a large number of pools that are connected to each other by ditches made of stone and sewers leading to the original irrigation system.

No one has yet understood how the Inca civilization can carry a huge stone weighing 20 tons to the top of Machu Picchu.
